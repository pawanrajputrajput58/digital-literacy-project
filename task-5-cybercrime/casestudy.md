# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI & Online Payment Fraud  
**Scenario Type:** Realistic Fictional Scenario (based on widely reported fraud patterns in India)

---

## The Crime

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in
India. Fraudsters exploit the simplicity and trust associated with apps like GPay,
PhonePe, and Paytm to deceive victims into authorising payments — often believing they
are *receiving* money, not sending it.

---

## How It Happened — Step by Step

**Victim:** Priya, a 20-year-old college student in Bhopal, listed her old laptop for
₹12,000 on OLX.

1. A "buyer" named Rohit contacted her on WhatsApp, claiming to be an Army officer.
2. Rohit said he would pay via UPI and sent a ₹1 QR code to "verify" her account.
3. Priya scanned it — and unknowingly *sent* ₹1 instead of receiving it.
4. Rohit said the full ₹12,000 was "pending" and sent a second QR for ₹12,000 to
   "release" the payment.
5. Priya scanned again. ₹12,000 was debited from her account instantly.
6. Rohit blocked her on all platforms. The money was gone within minutes.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted

- College students and first-time online sellers
- People selling items on OLX, Quikr, or Facebook Marketplace
- Job seekers offered "registration fees" refunds
- Customers of e-commerce platforms expecting refunds

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss:** Priya lost ₹12,001 — nearly a month's pocket money.
- **Psychological impact:** Shame, self-blame, and anxiety about using digital payments.
- **Low recovery rate:** UPI transactions are instant and near-irreversible; recovery
  depends entirely on how quickly the fraud is reported to the bank and cybercrime portal.
- **Wider impact:** Erodes trust in India's digital payment ecosystem.
