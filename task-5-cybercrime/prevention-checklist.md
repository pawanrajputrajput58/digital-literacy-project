# ✅ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | Digital Ambassador Initiative

---

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A good password is 12+ characters
      with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager like Bitwarden
      (free) so you don't have to remember them all.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and banking
      apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorised access.

- [ ] **Never click links in unsolicited SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages** — even if
      they appear to be from HDFC, SBI, Amazon, or IRCTC. Always go directly to the
      official website or app instead.

- [ ] **Check the URL before entering any login or payment details.** Fake sites often
      use addresses like `hdfcbank-login.net` or `paytm-refund.in`. The real domain
      must match exactly (e.g., `hdfcbank.com`).

- [ ] **Keep your phone and apps updated.** Security patches fix vulnerabilities that
      hackers actively exploit. Turn on automatic updates for your OS and apps.

- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi (college canteen, bus stand, café) for banking or
      logging into personal accounts.** If necessary, use a VPN (ProtonVPN has a free
      tier) to encrypt your connection.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory — 2 Tips)

- [ ] **🚨 CRITICAL — UPI is for SENDING, not receiving.**
      You do NOT need to scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money.
      If anyone asks you to scan or enter a PIN to "accept" a payment, it is 100% a
      scam. Stop immediately and block the contact.

- [ ] **🚨 Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app (most apps allow this
      under Settings → UPI → Daily Limit). Keeping it at ₹5,000–₹10,000 limits your
      maximum possible loss if you are defrauded.

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, CVV, or net banking password** with anyone —
      including people claiming to be bank employees, customer care executives, or
      government officials. No legitimate organisation ever asks for these.

- [ ] **Verify before paying.** When paying a shopkeeper or peer, confirm the name that
      appears on the payment screen before hitting 'Pay'. Fraudsters swap QR codes on
      donation boxes and small shops.

---

## 🌐 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Audit your social media privacy settings every semester.** Your phone number,
      date of birth, and home city should never be publicly visible — these are the
      building blocks of identity theft.

- [ ] **Do not accept friend/follow requests from unknown people.** Fake profiles are
      used for romance scams, sextortion, and data harvesting.

- [ ] **Think before posting.** Photos with your college ID, Aadhaar card, marksheets,
      or location tags can be used for fraud. Once posted, you cannot fully delete
      something from the internet.

---

## 🆘 Report It — Do Not Stay Silent

If you or someone you know is a victim of cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file a complaint online 24/7 |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — call immediately after fraud; faster bank action within the first hour |
| 🏦 **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call the number on the back of your debit/credit card |
| 👮 **Local Police** | Visit your nearest police station and file an FIR for serious cases |

> **Golden Rule:** Report within 1 hour of a UPI/payment fraud. Banks can freeze the
> recipient account and reverse transactions if reported fast enough.
